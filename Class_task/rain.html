Introduction

The rainy season in Chennai plays a very important role in the city’s climate and water supply. Unlike many other parts of India that depend mainly on the Southwest Monsoon, Chennai receives most of its rainfall from the Northeast Monsoon, which occurs between October and December. During this time, moist winds from the Bay of Bengal bring heavy showers, sometimes accompanied by cyclones. The rainfall not only cools down the hot weather but also replenishes lakes, reservoirs, and groundwater, which are essential for drinking water and agriculture. However, the rainy season also brings challenges such as flooding, traffic disruptions, and waterlogging in many parts of the city. Despite these issues, the rains are considered a lifeline for Chennai, helping the city prepare for the dry summer months.

Impacts In Daily Life

Impact on Daily Life The rainy season in Chennai has a significant impact on daily life. Heavy and sudden showers often lead to waterlogging on streets, making commuting difficult and slowing down traffic. Public transportation and office timings are frequently affected, and schools may sometimes close during intense rains. The storms and high winds can cause electricity cuts, fallen trees, and property damage. In addition, stagnant water after rains increases the risk of mosquito-borne diseases and other health issues. Despite these challenges, the rains provide relief from the hot weather and are essential for replenishing water sources that sustain the city throughout the year.

Reasons For Rainfall

The main reason for rainfall in Chennai is the Northeast Monsoon, which occurs between October and December. During this season, winds from the Bay of Bengal carry a large amount of moisture and move towards the Tamil Nadu coast, causing heavy showers. Unlike most parts of India that receive rain from the Southwest Monsoon, Chennai depends more on the Northeast Monsoon for its annual rainfall. In addition, cyclones and depressions formed over the Bay of Bengal often bring intense downpours and storms to the city.
